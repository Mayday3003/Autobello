About the observed behavior

By carrying out a point-by-point analysis of the data, several interesting observations can be made. Firstly, it can be seen how linear regression has a direct relationship with both the x and y variables. This means that as one variable increases, so does the other variable, and vice versa. Additionally, there is a particular case that is worth examining in detail: in one of the houses with four residents, a consumption of 239 KWh is recorded, exceeding even a property with a larger number of residents, where 221 KWh is consumed. This suggests that the house with four inhabitants either has excessive consumption, or conversely, that the property with six people has very low consumption.
Furthermore, it is possible to highlight that each property has a unique energy consumption range, which appears to be largely influenced by the number of residents living in the property. For example, in properties with two residents, energy consumption ranges between 115 and 137 KWh. In houses with three inhabitants, the range extends from 127 to 222 KWh. In properties with four residents, consumption varies between 110 and 239 KWh, while in houses with five inhabitants, consumption is 192 KWh. Finally, in the house with six residents, consumption is 221 KWh.
It is important to note that these variations in consumption ranges can be attributed to a variety of factors. For instance, differences in household size, behavior, and lifestyle can all impact energy consumption. Additionally, the age and efficiency of appliances, as well as insulation and heating systems, can also play a role in the amount of energy consumed by each household.
